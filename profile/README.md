## Why Baobab

The baobab is Africa's "Tree of Life" — a gathering point, a store of value,
a thing that survives drought and outlives generations. That's the model
we're building toward: open financial and identity infrastructure on
Stellar/Soroban that's resilient, community-owned, and useful whether
you're a market trader in Lagos or a smallholder farmer in Kaduna.

Most of our first repos start from a Nigerian problem, because that's
where we live and understand the pain points best. A couple are built to
scale to any emerging or agrarian economy from day one.

## What we're building

| Repo | What it does | Reach |
|---|---|---|
| [`diaspora-circle`](../../diaspora-circle) | Open Soroban protocol for cross-border Ajo/Esusu (rotating savings) circles — let diaspora members contribute in USDC/XLM, settle in seconds | Nigeria + diaspora |
| [`farmchain-receipts`](../../farmchain-receipts) | Tokenized warehouse receipts so smallholder farmers can use stored crops as verifiable collateral for financing | Nigeria, West Africa |
| [`remit-last-mile`](../../remit-last-mile) | Agent-network layer connecting Stellar-settled remittances to real cash-out points | Nigeria–UK/US, expandable |
| [`credverify`](../../credverify) | Tamper-proof academic & professional credential attestations, verifiable instantly by any employer | Global |
| [`harvestguard`](../../harvestguard) | Parametric crop micro-insurance with automatic payouts triggered by weather-oracle data | Africa + tropical/agrarian economies globally |

## Principles

- **Open by default** — protocols, not walled-garden apps
- **Built for the unbanked first** — low fees and offline-friendly flows are not an afterthought
- **Honest about maturity** — we label what's a working contract vs. a prototype
